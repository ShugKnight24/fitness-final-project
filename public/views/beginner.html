5 x 5

A great starter routine for those new to fitness is a 5 x 5 routine. It will allow you to get accustomed to most exercises while allowing you to make fairly linear progress. This program will allow you to build a solid foundation to build upon in the future.

The way you would approach this routine is to find weights that you feel comfortable completing 5 repititions for 5 sets. This may mean starting a bit lighter than you normally would. This will help to ensure that you complete all of your sets. Each following workout, you would add 5 pounds to each of the 5x5 exercises. If you feel comfortable increasing by more, go ahead, but. For accessory exercises, increase weight as you feel comfortable.

Warming up with the bar on all the exercises and slowly build up to your working sets. Warm ups do not count in the amount of sets you have done.

If you are unable to complete the 5x5, that is okay, complete it for as many reps and sets as you can. Try that weight again the next time you would perform that exercise. If you succeed in hitting 5x5, move up in weight the following workout. If you fail to hit 5x5 again, the next workout, you would reduce the weight you used by 10% and start building back up from there. If the number you get ends with a decimal, round down. Finally, only reduce the weight in the exercise you failed in.

Workout Routine A
  • Squats 5x5
  • Bench Press 5x5
  • Bent over rows 5x5
  • Barbell Shrugs 3x8-12
  • Tricep exercise of your choice 3x8-12
  • Bicep exercise of your choice 3x8-12
  • Pull-ups until you can hit 5x5, if too easy use weight
  • Rope Cruches – 3x10-20
Workout Routine B
  • Squats 5x5
  • Deadlift 1x5
  • Overhead press 5x5
  • Bent over rows 5x5
  • Tricep exercise of your choice 3x8-12
  • Bicep exercise of your choice 3x8-12
  • Chin-up until you can hit 5x5, if too easy use weight
  • Rope Crunch 3x10-20

If you start your routine on Monday this is how the program would look.

  • Monday – Workout A
  • Tuesday – Off Day
  • Wednesday – Workout B
  • Thursday – Off Day
  • Friday – Workout A
  • Saturday – Off Day
  • Sunday – Off Day

    • The following Monday you would start with workout B and alternate to workout A and so on.
